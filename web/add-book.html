Add a Book

On this page you can add a new book to your book collection.

Step 1: Lookup by ISBN

This step is optional, so you can choose to skip it if you want. If you'd like to add book details by yourself, or if the book you're looking to add doesn't have an ISBN number, please move on to the next step.

Enter the ISBN of the book you want to add. Using external sources, The Book Project will attempt to retrieve information about this book, and pre-fill the form fields below.

An ISBN is a unique number used to identify a specific version or edition of a book. Before 2007, books were assigned 10-digit ISBNs, but since 2007, all books use 13-digit ISBNs. The 13-digit ISBN often also serves as the book’s barcode. You can usually find the ISBN on the back cover of the book, near the barcode.

How We Retrieve Book Information:

We collect book information using external data sources like Google Books and Open Library. We then use AI to integrate and organise this data to automatically fill in the form fields below. For more information on how we use AI, please see our Privacy Policy.

Important Notice:

The information we obtain from these sources may not always be accurate, and some details might be missing. Feel free to modify any retrieved information before saving the book.

Step 2: Enter Book Details

Here you can enter the general details about this book (title, subtitle, etc.). These details describe the work itself, not a specific physical copy. In a later step, you’ll be able to add information about the individual copies you own.

Understanding Our Book System:

In this system, a Book refers to a specific edition of a work, usually uniquely identified by its ISBN. For example, the hardcover edition and the paperback edition of The Fellowship of the Ring are considered different books.

A Book Copy refers to an individual copy of one of those editions that you personally own. You might own two paperback copies of the same edition, or one paperback and one hardcover (different ISBNs).
